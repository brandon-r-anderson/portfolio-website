About Me

Biology graduate, aspiring data analyst, and lifelong collector of unnecessary amounts of information.

I've always liked having as much relevant information as possible before making decisions. Whether I'm comparing products, planning a home improvement project, experimenting in the kitchen, or considering career options, I prefer understanding not just what works, but why it works. I don't enjoy feeling like I'm missing important pieces of information or relying solely on intuition when evidence is available.

That tendency is what drew me toward data analytics. I like collecting information, organizing it, looking for patterns, and finding ways to make it easier to understand. Messy datasets don't bother me as much as they make me curious. I often find myself wondering what relationships exist beneath the surface, what factors are actually influencing outcomes, and how different pieces of information fit together into a larger picture.

I graduated from Brigham Young University with a B.S. in Biology. Although my interests have gradually shifted toward analytics, biology remains one of my deepest interests. Studying biology strengthened qualities that I already seemed to have naturally: curiosity, analytical thinking, and a desire to understand how things work. Over time, that curiosity expanded into many other subjects and hobbies as well.

To develop my analytical skills, I've been building projects with SQL, Excel, Power BI, Python, and Git. I'm still discovering which areas of analytics interest me most, but I enjoy the broader process of taking information that is scattered or incomplete and turning it into something clearer and more useful.

On a personal level, I value curiosity, intellectual honesty, and continual learning. I try to approach both work and relationships with the understanding that everyone has something to teach and something to learn. I appreciate environments where people enjoy helping one another improve, asking good questions, and working together toward better answers.

I'm currently seeking an entry-level Data Analyst position where I can continue developing technically, contribute meaningful work, and learn alongside thoughtful and supportive colleagues.
